What is CKD?

Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD) means your kidneys are damaged and can't filter blood the way they should. This damage happens slowly over months or years. Waste builds up in your body and can cause serious health problems.

Silent disease – You may not feel symptoms until kidneys are close to failing.

How kidneys work

Your two kidneys, each the size of a fist, filter 120-150 quarts of blood daily. They remove waste and extra water to make urine. They also control blood pressure, keep bones healthy, and make red blood cells.

Causes & Risk Factors

Main causes

  • Diabetes (38%)
  • High blood pressure (26%)
  • Glomerulonephritis
  • Polycystic kidney disease

Risk factors

  • Family history
  • Age 60+
  • Heart disease
  • Smoking, NSAIDs

High-risk groups

  • African American
  • Hispanic
  • Asian, Native American
  • Autoimmune diseases

Signs & Symptoms

Early CKD often has no symptoms. As it progresses:

  • Fatigue, trouble concentrating
  • Swollen feet/ankles
  • Puffiness around eyes
  • Dry, itchy skin
  • Muscle cramps
  • Urinating more at night
  • Nausea, loss of appetite
  • Shortness of breath
  • Metallic taste
  • Trouble sleeping
